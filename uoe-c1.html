Part 1 🔘 Multiple Choice Cloze
Part 1 — Multiple Choice Cloze. Read the text and choose the best answer (A, B, C or D) for each gap. Eight questions, 1 mark each.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 1▾
Strategy
💡At C1, many questions test subtle differences between near-synonyms. Think about connotation, register, and collocation — not just meaning.
💡Pay attention to dependent prepositions and phrasal verbs — these are heavily tested at this level.
Common Mistakes
⚠️Beware of options that are grammatically correct but don't collocate naturally with the surrounding words.
⚠️Read beyond the immediate gap. Sometimes the answer depends on a word later in the sentence or paragraph.
Time
⏱️About 8–10 minutes. Trust your instinct on collocations — if it sounds right, it probably is.

Part 2 ✏️ Open Cloze
Part 2 — Open Cloze. Read the text and think of the word which best fits each gap. Use only one word in each gap.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 2▾
Strategy
💡C1 open cloze often tests advanced grammar: relative clauses, participle clauses, inversion, and complex connectors.
💡Some answers are content words in fixed phrases. Don't assume every answer is a grammar word.
Common Mistakes
⚠️Read the full paragraph, not just the sentence. Discourse-level coherence often determines the answer.
⚠️Watch for "obvious" answers that don't quite fit the grammatical slot — C1 tests precision.
Time
⏱️About 10–12 minutes. The difficulty is higher than B2, so budget accordingly.

Part 3 🔤 Word Formation
Part 3 — Word Formation. Read the text below. Use the word given in capitals at the end of some of the lines to form a word that fits in the gap in the same line.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 3▾
Strategy
💡Expect more complex transformations at C1: double changes (prefix + suffix), internal spelling changes, and less common derivations.
💡Consider whether the sentence needs a positive or negative meaning — negative prefixes (un-, in-, dis-, mis-) are commonly tested.
Common Mistakes
⚠️Spelling is critical. At C1, you're expected to know tricky forms like "accommodation", "occurrence", "consciousness".
⚠️Don't assume the most common form is correct. Read the sentence — a less obvious derivative may be needed.
Time
⏱️About 10 minutes. If unsure, write your best attempt and move on.

Part 4 🔑 Key Word Transformations
Part 4 — Key Word Transformations. Complete the second sentence so that it has a similar meaning to the first, using the word given. Do not change the word given.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 4▾
Strategy
💡C1 transformations test advanced structures: cleft sentences, mixed conditionals, advanced passives, and idiomatic expressions.
💡The key word may be part of a fixed phrase or idiom. Think laterally — what expression uses this word?
Common Mistakes
⚠️You need between 3 and 6 words. Both sentences must convey identical meaning, including emphasis and attitude.
⚠️Don't just rearrange the words. The transformation usually requires a structural change (e.g. active → passive, direct → reported).
Time
⏱️Allow 15 minutes. Each question carries 2 marks (1 for each half), so partial credit is possible.
