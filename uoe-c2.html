Part 1 Multiple Choice Cloze
Part 1 — Multiple Choice Cloze. Read the text and choose the best answer (A, B, C or D) for each gap. Eight questions, 1 mark each.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 1▾
Strategy
💡At C2, expect highly nuanced distinctions. The wrong options may be grammatically and semantically plausible — only collocation or register eliminates them.
💡Pay close attention to idiomatic expressions and fixed phrases that cannot be altered.
Common Mistakes
⚠️Avoid over-analysis. If you've narrowed it to two options, trust your feel for natural English.
⚠️Some options differ only in formality or connotation. Consider the register of the text.
Time
⏱️About 10 minutes. Flag uncertain answers and revisit if time permits.

Part 2 Open Cloze
Part 2 — Open Cloze. Read the text and think of the word which best fits each gap. Use only one word in each gap.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 2▾
Strategy
💡C2 open cloze tests sophisticated grammar: inversion, subjunctive, ellipsis, cleft structures, and advanced connectors.
💡Some answers are content words (nouns/adjectives) in fixed phrases. Don't assume every answer is a grammar word.
Common Mistakes
⚠️Read the full paragraph, not just the sentence. Discourse-level coherence often determines the answer.
⚠️At C2, "obvious" answers can be traps. Double-check that the word fits the precise grammatical slot.
Time
⏱️About 10–12 minutes. The difficulty is higher, so budget slightly more time.

Part 3 Word Formation
Part 3 — Word Formation. Read the text below. Use the word given in capitals at the end of some of the lines to form a word that fits in the gap in the same line.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 3▾
Strategy
💡Expect rare and complex transformations: double prefixes, uncommon suffixes, and internal vowel changes.
💡Some prompt words require a derivation from a different word class entirely (e.g. verb → abstract noun of a different root).
Common Mistakes
⚠️Spelling is critical and there are no half marks. Practise writing out difficult words by hand.
⚠️Don't assume the most common derivative is correct. Read the sentence carefully — a less obvious form may be needed.
Time
⏱️About 10–12 minutes. If unsure, write your best attempt and move on.

Part 4 Key Word Transformations
Part 4 — Key Word Transformations. Complete the second sentence so that it has a similar meaning to the first, using the word given. Do not change the word given.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 4▾
Strategy
💡C2 transformations test the full range: idioms, advanced passives, subjunctive, complex inversion, and cleft sentences.
💡The key word may be part of an idiom or fixed expression. Think laterally — what phrase uses this word?
Common Mistakes
⚠️You need between 3 and 8 words at C2. Longer answers are expected — don't try to be too concise.
⚠️Both sentences must convey identical meaning including emphasis, attitude, and implied meaning.
Time
⏱️Allow 15–18 minutes. This part carries the most marks per question, so invest the time.
