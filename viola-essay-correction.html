Task Write an essay for your tutor discussing two reasons why young people commit crimes (lack of parental control / influence of friends). Explain which is most important and give reasons. 220–260 words.
Flagged Corrected

Hardly a week goes by without another report of underage crime appearing in the media. In order to prevent the consistency of this phenomenon, it is essential to come face to face with two of the possible causes lying behind it — the lack of control by the family and the role played by peers. This essay argues that, although every factor matters, the first cause ultimately prevails.

Research indicates that is adolescence the main phase in which most episodes of crime start to arise. This cannot be taken as a surprise, since it coincides with a less control exercised by superiors and with the young stepping into the real world, in which they will encounter people from a wide range of backgrounds. However, the worst reports involve school dropout or cyberbullying, too serious to be linked just to a couple of schoolmates. It should, as a result, lie in the environment in which teenagers grow up: the more carefully it is taken care of, the few will be the variables that may lead him to commit crimes.

It becomes clear, then, that this kind of influence can be prevented by parents, who hold the rains from the beginning and are in charge to build the foundation for their children's future. This can be exemplified not only by giving them a proper education through school, but also by dedicating the right amount of attention and affection, which cannot be underestimated when it comes to developing one's chief values. This is why even an apparently innocent misbehaviour, such as shoplifting, often hide lack fo supervision or complete neglecting.

In conclusion, even if a teenager falls under a bad influence throughout his path, how it will ultimately affect his possibility to commit a crime is to be ascribed to an inner set of morals, which are originally established by the parents and their educational efforts.

Task Write an essay discussing two reasons why the arts are important in modern society (need for escapism / appreciating beauty / cost of funding). Explain which is most important and give reasons. 220–260 words.
Flagged Corrected

The 21st century is proving to be a time of huge change in society, especially as regards arts and entertainment industries. The two used to be under the spotlight many decades ago, but now they are losing their spark as they are no longer seen as valuable sources. This essay is aimed to underlight their positive impact on people, which seems to be irrelevant to those who only sees them as a waste of funds.

The widespread view among the cynical ones is that, indeed, there is no need of art galleries or theatres in modern society. Alongside this, these people also lament their maintenance to be too expensive and that the money should be invested on sports facilities. What they are forgetting is that, however, our culture and customs only exist thanks to arts development, which is also what makes a country different from the others.

It becomes clear, then, that the main reason why we we'll always need art in each of its declination is because it represents a solid point of reference for whenever we feel confused or face a period of difficulty, allowing us to escape from reality, going backwards in time. Humans have in fact relied on art ever since stone age in order to express their feelings, which are universal and timeless.

Task Write an essay discussing two ways individuals can make a difference to environmental issues (avoid plastic / raise awareness / choose how to travel). Explain which is more effective and give reasons. 220–260 words.
Flagged Corrected

Amid ongoing social and technological change, few issues are as relevant as environmental one. Our planet getting hotter and hotter is a threat to future lives, and only our habits can put the brakes on it. This essay examines whether greater emphasis should be placed on plastic avoidance or on alternative ways of transportation.

It is generally considered that gas emissions are to be blamed on the gasoline used to fuel cars. Therefore, many government policies have tried to push people to travel in alternative ways: to give just one illustration, the introduction of restricted areas where only certain vehicles are allowed. Although this may undoubtedly reduce the pollution, banning cars altogether is a rather unviable option, due to the fact that most people can only rely on their own car to get to work.

Beyond this, a more consequential issue emerges: the gigantic plastic consumption. In fact, its combustion pollutes as much as cars do. Coupled with this, not only does plastic affect our health with its poisonous emissions: its massive presence damages the lives of all ecosystems, being them underwater or above ground. Government should therefore focus on measures to prevent such use of plastic, but at the end of the day it does not take huge effort to separate plastic from the other rubbish, to opt for a steel bottle or a reusable bag to do the groceries.

In conclusion, as it is unthinkable to circulate without cars, becoming more conscious of our use of plastic only requires a few small habits in our daily lives, which will certainly have a meaningful impact on the environment.

✏️ Tips for Viola — C1 Advanced Essay Writing

📐
Keep a clear four-paragraph structure Introduction (thesis) → Body 1 (point 1) → Body 2 (point 2 + comparison) → Conclusion. All three of your essays drift between ideas within paragraphs. Each body paragraph should have one clear focus, developed and then linked back to your overall argument.
🎯
State your thesis clearly — and argue it throughout Your introductions are confident and well-signposted, which is a real strength. Make sure the argument promised in the introduction is actually developed and resolved in the body paragraphs, not just restated in the conclusion.
"This essay argues that, while X cannot be dismissed, Y is ultimately the more significant factor for the following reasons..."
⚖️
Compare your two points explicitly All three tasks ask you to decide which point is more important or effective. Don't just describe both — weigh them against each other using comparative language.
"While changing travel habits requires systemic infrastructure, reducing plastic use demands only small, immediate changes at individual level — making it the more achievable and therefore more impactful option."
🔗
Use precise academic linking language Avoid informal connectors. Upgrade to higher-register alternatives that signal logical relationships more precisely.
❌ "at the end of the day" → ✅ "ultimately" / "in practice"
❌ "What they are forgetting is that, however" → ✅ "However, what is frequently overlooked is that..."
📚
Paraphrase the discussion prompts — don't ignore them The task says to use your own words as far as possible. Rephrase the opinions and build on them rather than lifting them directly. This shows range and earns marks for vocabulary.
Prompt: "It's impossible to change the way we travel."
"While a wholesale shift away from car travel may be unrealistic given existing infrastructure, individuals can still make meaningful choices about how they travel day to day."
🧠
Watch for Italian interference (false friends and calques) Several errors across the three essays come from Italian structures mapped directly onto English. The most common patterns to watch for:
circolare → "get around / travel" (not "circulate")
declinazione → "form / type" (not "declination")
fare la spesa → "do the shopping" (not "do the groceries")
investire su → "invest in" (not "invest on")
🔢
Use they/their for generic singular reference When referring to a teenager, an individual, or a person generically, use they/their throughout — not his. This is both grammatically correct and natural in contemporary academic English. This error appears in all three essays.
✅
What you're already doing well Your introductions are structured and confident. Your vocabulary range is genuinely strong — words like ascribed to, prevails, consequential, misbehaviour show real C1 awareness. You use complex sentence structures effectively and your conclusions are often your best paragraphs. The work now is to bring the body paragraphs up to the same standard: tighter logic, consistent register, and no Italian interference.
