Route A: The Galician Loop

Santiago de Compostela → Costa da Morte → Cíes Islands → North Galicia → Asturias → Picos de Europa → return

📍 Base: Santiago de Compostela (SCQ airport) ~950 km total driving 2 proper rest days 2 must-book-ahead spots

This route covers Galicia's wild, Celtic coast and the rugged green mountains of Asturias. Driving days are kept short — two full rest days are built in at the Cíes Islands and the Picos de Europa area, so it's not a blur of movement. The return swing inland through Ribeira Sacra and Lugo adds something completely different.

1Day
Arrival — Santiago de Compostela
🏛 Culture 🍷 Galician food 🟢 Easy day
▾
🏛
Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela
One of Europe's most extraordinary Romanesque buildings, and the spiritual endpoint of the Camino pilgrimage. Arrive in Praza do Obradoiro in the morning — the light on the twin towers is magnificent. Entry is free; book the rooftop tour ahead if you want the elevated view (€12). The Pórtico da Gloria inside is a masterpiece of medieval sculpture.
🕘 09:30 – 12:00 · Free · Rooftop tour ~€12
🍽
Lunch on Rúa do Franco
The pedestrian lanes south of the Cathedral are lined with restaurants. Order pulpo á feira (octopus with paprika and olive oil on a wooden plate), empanada gallega, and a cold Albariño. Menú del día is ~€13–16 and usually excellent. No rushing — this is the arrival day.
🕐 13:00 – 15:00
🎒
Afternoon wander — Mercado de Abastos
Santiago's covered market is one of the best in Galicia. Stone stalls, women selling goat's cheese from baskets, fresh percebes (barnacles), jars of Padrón peppers. A good place to stock up the van larder too.
🕒 16:00 – 18:00 · Free
🚐
Camping As Lagoas (near Santiago)
Reliable, well-reviewed campsite just outside the city. Good hook-up pitches, clean showers. Short drive from the centre. Use the large city-edge car parks and walk in by day; sleep out here.
🕗 19:00 onwards · ~€18–22/night
💡 Parking: The city centre is largely pedestrianised. Park at Praza de Galicia or the San Lázaro car park and walk 10–12 minutes in. Do not attempt to drive into the old quarter with the van.

2Day
Costa da Morte — Fisterra & Muxía
🚐 ~90 km 🌊 Wild coast 🏰 Lighthouse
▾
🌊
Cape Finisterre (Cabo Fisterra)
The westernmost point of continental Spain — known as "the end of the world" for centuries. A lighthouse sits at km 0 of the Camino, and pilgrims traditionally burn their boots here. Walk to the very tip and look west at pure open Atlantic. The sunsets, if you can stay for them, are extraordinary — come back in the evening if your campsite is close.
🕙 10:00 – 12:30 · Free · 1h 20min from Santiago
🏖
Praia de Langosteira
A 3 km beach just north of Fisterra town, sheltered from the heaviest Atlantic swell and excellent for swimming. Fine golden sand, rarely crowded. Perfect for a late morning dip before lunch.
🕛 12:30 – 14:00
🪨
Muxía — Santuario da Virxe da Barca
A Romanesque sanctuary perched on an exposed headland of ancient granite boulders, where the Atlantic hammers in from the west. Pre-Christian in feel and profoundly atmospheric. The rocking stones (Pedra de Abalar) have been here since before the church was built. 30 min from Fisterra.
🕒 15:30 – 17:00 · Free
🚐
Portosín Camper Park
Exceptional camper park with panoramic bay views, rated 4.9⭐ from 360+ reviews. Owner greets arrivals personally. Walking distance to beaches. Café on site. One of the best in Galicia.
🕕 18:30 · ~€13–16/night
🚐 Santiago → Fisterra: 90 km, 1h 20min 📍 Fisterra → Muxía: 30 km, 35 min 📍 → Portosín: ~55 km, 50 min
💡 Road note: Costa da Morte roads are narrow, winding, and beautiful. Budget extra time, drive slowly, and enjoy them. Pick up fresh bread and local cheese in Fisterra for a clifftop picnic.
🗺 Google Maps
3Day
Cíes Islands — Full Day Rest Day
🏝 No driving 🤿 Swimming ⚠️ Book well ahead
▾
⛴
Ferry from Vigo → Cíes Islands (~35 min)
Drive to Vigo (40 min from Portosín area) and take the ferry across to the islands. The approach by sea is spectacular — three islands that form the outer wall of the Rías Baixas. Park the van in Vigo's harbour area. Check mardeons.es for schedules.
🕗 Depart 08:30 · First ferry usually 09:30 · Return ferry ~18:30
🏝
Playa de Rodas — Cíes Islands
Consistently voted one of the world's best beaches. White sand, a sheltered turquoise lagoon, pristine crystal-clear water, and untouched national park forest backing it. Snorkel the rocks on the southern end for extraordinary marine life. Bring your own snorkel — none available on the island.
🕙 All day
🦅
Hike to Monte Faro Lighthouse (optional)
A 2 hour return hike through pine forest to the northern lighthouse. Views over the whole Rías Baixas are extraordinary. The island hosts one of Europe's largest yellow-legged gull colonies — the noise and scale of it are genuinely impressive. Do this in the morning before the beach.
🕙 09:30 – 11:30 · Then beach for the afternoon
🦪
Vigo oysters on the way back — Rúa Pescadería
After the return ferry, Vigo's old fish market street (Rúa Pescadería / "la Berbería") sells the best oysters in Spain — straight off ice, served with lemon on newspaper. About €1 each. Do not skip this.
🕕 19:00 – 20:00
⚠️ Booking is essential: The Cíes Islands require a free online permit (strictly capped daily visitors) plus ferry tickets. In July/August these go weeks in advance. Do this before planning anything else.
🗺 Google Maps
4Day
North to As Catedrais
🚐 ~220 km 🏖 Iconic beach ⚠️ Tidal timing
▾
🛣
Morning drive north — optional A Coruña stop
Take the AP-9 north along the Galician coast. If time allows, A Coruña is worth a coffee stop — the Torre de Hércules (world's oldest working Roman lighthouse) is just outside the city, and the old town has a beautiful waterfront. But keep an eye on the time: your As Catedrais ticket will be timed to low tide.
🕗 Depart early from Vigo area
🏖
As Catedrais — Playa de las Catedrales
One of Spain's most spectacular beaches — Gothic-arch rock formations carved by the Atlantic, accessible only 2–3 hours either side of low tide. The caves and arches are genuinely awe-inspiring. Rated 4.7⭐ with 11,000+ reviews. "Full satisfaction guaranteed," "worth every extra mile." Only accessible via a free timed ticket — book via playadelascatedrales.gal (time your visit to low tide on the day).
🕛 Timed by low tide · ~2 hours at beach · Free
🚐
A Frouxeira Camper Park — Valdoviño
Excellent camper area near the A Frouxeira lagoon and surf beach. 4.6⭐, 459+ reviews. €16/night with electric, water, waste disposal. The beach below is stunning. Automated plate-reading entry system — very efficient. The lagoon is an SPA wildlife sanctuary (flamingos and waders).
🕔 16:30 onwards
🚐 Vigo → A Coruña: ~150 km, 1h 45min 📍 A Coruña → As Catedrais: ~80 km, 55 min 📍 → A Frouxeira: 20 km, 20 min
⚠️ Tidal timing is everything: Check low tide at Ribadeo on the day of your visit. Going anywhere near high tide means the arches are underwater and there's nothing to see. Your ticket slot will reflect this — plan the rest of the day's drive around it.
🗺 Google Maps
5Day
Into Asturias — Llanes & Ribadesella
🚐 ~130 km ⛵ Harbour towns 🏄 Surf
▾
🏰
Llanes — Walled Medieval Town
A beautifully preserved medieval walled town on the Asturian coast. The harbourside is lined with pintxo bars serving extraordinary food — try oricios (sea urchin) and local cider. The Bufones de Pría blowholes nearby are spectacular in heavy swell. An excellent stop for a long lunch.
🕙 10:00 – 13:30
⛵
Ribadesella
A lovely estuary town at the mouth of the Sella river. The old harbour town has character, the beach across the bridge is long, and if you book ahead, the Cueva de Tito Bustillo (20,000-year-old cave paintings, €8) is one of the finest prehistoric art sites in the world. Strictly limited daily visitors — book via titobustillo.com.
🕒 14:30 – 17:30
🚐
Camp near Ribadesella or Colunga
Camping Las Arenas de Cabrales is excellent. Alternatively, Camping Playa de Vega near Colunga — right above a perfect wild beach.
🕗 18:30 · ~€18–24/night
🚐 Valdoviño → Llanes: ~185 km, 2h 20min 📍 Llanes → Ribadesella: 30 km, 30 min 📌 Take coast roads where possible — they're slow but beautiful
🗺 Google Maps
6Day
Picos de Europa — Fuente Dé & Hike Rest Base
⛰ Base 2 nights 🚐 60 km to park 🦅 Mountains
▾
⛰
Fuente Dé Cable Car
Rated 4.8⭐ national park with 17,000+ reviews. The cable car rises 753m in 4 minutes to 1,823m altitude — extraordinary views open instantly. From the top, walk to Mirador del Cable (easy, 20 min), or take the longer trail to the Vega de Liordes for complete isolation and Iberian chamois. Book ahead in summer — queues of 2–3 hours without a ticket. cantur.com
🕗 Arrive 08:00 to beat queues · Cable car €20 return · Hike 3–4 hours
🧀
Arenas de Cabrales — Cabrales Blue Cheese
The village of Arenas de Cabrales is the epicentre of one of Spain's great artisan cheeses — cave-aged blue cheese sold direct from local producers at the village market. Sharp, powerful, extraordinary with honey. A jar of local honey from the same market is perfect van food.
🕐 Afternoon · Village market
🚐
Camping Picos de Europa — stay 2 nights
Set yourself up here for two nights — no need to pack up tomorrow. The campsite is inside the park boundary with mountain views, good facilities, and trails from the door. Having a fixed base makes Day 7 completely relaxed.
🏕 2-night pitch · ~€20–26/night
🚐 Ribadesella → Fuente Dé: ~65 km, 1h 10min 📌 Drive via Panes → stunning Desfiladero de la Hermida gorge
💡 The gorge: The Desfiladero de la Hermida is a spectacular narrow river gorge en route to Fuente Dé — vertical walls over 1,500m high. Drive slowly through it. There are river pools where you can swim in summer — check as you pass.
🗺 Google Maps
7Day
Picos — Lakes, Villages & Cabo de Peñas Rest Day
🟢 No packing up 🌊 Coastal cliffs 🏔 Lakes
▾
🏔
Covadonga Lakes — Lagos de Enol & Ercina
Two glacial lakes set in the high mountains above the Covadonga Sanctuary. In summer, cars are banned — take the shuttle bus from the car park below (€12 return). The views across the peaks reflected in the lakes are otherworldly. Walk the lakeside circuit (1.5 hours, easy) and have a coffee at the mountain refugio.
🕗 08:30 – 12:00 · Shuttle €12
🌊
Cabo de Peñas
Asturias's northernmost cape — raw cliffs, an old lighthouse, and huge green Atlantic rollers smashing below. Rated 4.8⭐. "Best spot in Asturias." The short walk to the cliff edge from the car park is extraordinary. No crowds, no entrance fee, no infrastructure — just the wild coast.
🕒 14:00 – 16:30 · Free · ~45 min from Covadonga
🍺
Evening sidrería in Gijón or Avilés (optional drive)
If energy allows, Gijón is 45 min north — a real Asturian city with a great beach (Playa de San Lorenzo) and excellent cider culture. Sidra is poured from shoulder height into wide glasses to aerate it. Any sidrería will do this theatrically. Pair with cachopo (breaded veal stuffed with ham and cheese).
🕗 Evening (optional)
💡 Today is genuinely relaxed. You're sleeping in the same spot, so there's no packing, no driving with everything on, no route to plan. Pick two of the three stops above depending on mood. This is the day to do nothing if nothing feels right.
🗺 Google Maps
8Day
Lugo Roman Walls & Ribeira Sacra
🚐 ~180 km 🏛 UNESCO walls 🍷 Canyon & vineyards
▾
🏰
Lugo — Roman Walls
Lugo has the world's only complete Roman city wall still standing — 2.2 km of perfectly preserved 3rd-century Roman ramparts you can walk along in their entirety. UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city inside is genuine and unpretentious. Uniquely for Spain, every bar in the historic centre includes free pinchos with every drink — it's built into local custom.
🕙 10:00 – 13:00 · Free
🍷
Ribeira Sacra — Sil Canyon Catamaran
A UNESCO Biosphere of dramatic river canyons with near-vertical terraced vineyards clinging to the walls — Mencía grapes grown on slopes so steep the harvest is done by hand and rope. The catamaran trip up the Sil Canyon from Santo Estevo (~€15, 1.5 hours) is one of the finest boat trips in Spain. Utterly spectacular. Book ahead in summer.
🕒 15:30 – 18:30 · navierasacra.es
🚐
Camp near O Carballiño or head back toward Santiago
Final camping night in Galicia's green interior. Stock up at a Mercadona and make a proper van dinner. You're about 1 hour from Santiago for tomorrow's final morning.
🕗 19:30
🚐 Picos → Lugo: ~140 km, 1h 50min 📍 Lugo → Ribeira Sacra: ~50 km, 45 min
🗺 Google Maps
9Day
Last Morning — Praia de Louro & Farewell Dinner
🎉 Last day 🏖 Wild beach 🍷 Santiago dinner
▾
🏖
Praia de Louro — Costa da Morte
One final wild Galician beach before returning to Santiago. Backed by a freshwater lagoon and dunes, with views of Monte Louro rising above. Rated 4.7⭐. A large picnic area makes it perfect for a farewell van breakfast al fresco. The water is cold and invigorating — ideal for a last dip.
🕙 10:00 – 13:00 · Free · ~45 min from Santiago
🥘
Farewell dinner in Santiago
Return to Santiago for a proper send-off. Head back to the area around Rúa do Franco or Rúa da Raíña. Order Galician beef (vaca galega), another round of pulpo, and a bottle of Albariño. The city in the evening — lit up, pilgrims in the square — is as good as it gets.
🕗 20:30 (Spain eats late — embrace it)
✈️ Getting home: Santiago de Compostela Airport (SCQ) has direct flights to Dublin, London Heathrow/Gatwick, Edinburgh, and other European cities. It's a small, easy airport — 15 min from the city centre.

Route B: Bilbao, Basque Coast & La Rioja

Bilbao → Basque coast (east) → Cantabria east → La Rioja inland → Laguardia → return to Bilbao

📍 Base: Bilbao (BIO airport) East of Somo only ~750 km total driving 2 proper rest days La Rioja inland loop

This route keeps entirely east of Somo, meaning the western Cantabrian coast (Santander, Comillas, Oyambre) is out — instead, you go east through the compact and extraordinary Basque coast (Gaztelugatxe, Mundaka, San Sebastián, Getaria, Flysch coast), then swing south into La Rioja wine country — Haro, Logroño, and the medieval hilltop village of Laguardia — before returning to Bilbao. Shorter distances, higher quality per km.

1Day
Arrival — Bilbao City Day
🟢 Easy day 🏛 Guggenheim 🍢 Pintxos
▾
🏛
Guggenheim Museum Bilbao
Frank Gehry's titanium-clad masterpiece on the Nervión river. Rated 4.5⭐ with 93,000+ reviews. The building itself — fluid, sculptural, constantly different depending on light — is one of the most extraordinary structures of the 20th century. World-class rotating contemporary art exhibitions inside. Closed Mondays. Book tickets in advance.
🕙 10:00 – 13:00 · Tue–Sun · ~€16 · Book online
🍢
Pintxo tour — Casco Viejo (7 Calles)
Bilbao's old town is one of the best places in Spain for pintxos — tiny, intricate bites of anchovy, cod, jamón, idiazábal cheese and many other combinations, served on bread and eaten at the bar. The method: one or two pintxos and a small txakoli wine per bar, then move on. Bar Bilbao, Bar Gatz, and El Globo are all excellent. Budget €2.50–3.50 per pintxo.
🕐 13:30 – 16:00
🌉
Puente Bizkaia — Vizcaya Bridge
The world's oldest transporter bridge — UNESCO World Heritage Site. A gondola car is suspended from a high span and carries passengers across the river at Getxo, about 20 min from central Bilbao. Utterly unique, slightly surreal, and brilliant. Walk the top gantry (€9) for the best views.
🕒 16:30 – 18:00 · Gondola €0.45 · Walk €9
🚐
Área de autocaravanas — Suesa / Somo area
Motorhomes Area Suesa (4.0⭐, 463 reviews) — a functional, level site with hook-up, water, waste disposal, €12/night. Somo beach (excellent surf) is a 2.5 km walk. Also the Somo–Santander foot ferry is 5 min away for a quick evening trip into Santander if desired.
🕗 19:00 · ~€12–16/night
💡 Van parking in Bilbao: Park near the Guggenheim (riverside car park) and walk across the bridge into Casco Viejo (~15 min). The city is very walkable. Don't try to drive through the old streets with the van.
🗺 Google Maps
2Day
Gaztelugatxe, Mundaka & Laga Rest Base
🟢 2 nights here 🏰 Island hermitage 🏄 Surf & swim
▾
🏰
San Juan de Gaztelugatxe
A tiny island connected to the mainland by a dramatic stone bridge and 241 steps. A 10th-century hermitage crowns the top. Appeared in Game of Thrones as Dragonstone. One of the most photographed places in Spain — and genuinely extraordinary, especially at dawn before crowds arrive. Timed access is strictly managed in summer. Ring the bell three times at the top for luck.
🕗 Arrive for opening time · Timed ticket essential — gaztelugatxe.eus
🏄
Mundaka — World-Class Surf
One of the longest and most powerful left-hand river-mouth barrel waves in the world. Even if the surf isn't firing, the fishing village is beautiful — white houses tumbling down to a small harbour, excellent bars, views across the Urdaibai estuary. Lunch at the harbour bars is relaxed and excellent.
🕛 12:00 – 14:30
🏖
Playa de Laga — Urdaibai Biosphere
A stunning beach within the UNESCO Urdaibai Biosphere Reserve. Mountains meet the sea, golden sand, consistent waves, crystal clear water. Rated 4.7⭐ — reviewers call it "one of the best beaches in the world." The mountain backdrop makes it feel like somewhere bigger than it is. Arrive mid-afternoon for the best light.
🕒 15:00 – 18:30 · Free
🚐
Camping Urdaibai — stay 2 nights
Park up here for both Day 2 and Day 3 — same pitch, no packing up. Inside the biosphere reserve, quiet and green, good facilities. Day 3 is completely unstructured from this base.
🏕 2-night pitch · ~€18–24/night
🚐 Suesa → Gaztelugatxe: ~90 km, 1h 10min 📍 → Mundaka: 15 min 📍 → Laga: 10 min
⚠️ Gaztelugatxe tickets: In July/August, timed tickets go weeks in advance. Check gaztelugatxe.eus as soon as dates are confirmed. The 09:00 slot is the best — fewer people, beautiful morning light.
🗺 Google Maps
3Day
Free Day — Urdaibai & Coast Rest Day
🟢 No driving needed 🦅 Bird reserve 🏊 Swimming
▾
☀️
Morning — however you want it
Sleep in. Coffee at the van. Walk down to Laga for an early swim while it's quiet. Read a book on the beach. This is the day with no agenda. The Urdaibai estuary has brilliant birdlife (egrets, spoonbills, and a huge variety of waders) — if anyone has binoculars, the reserve is superb.
🕐 Unstructured morning
🐟
Long lunch in Gernika
Gernika (Guernica) is 15 min from Laga — famous as the town destroyed by Nazi bombing in 1937 (commemorated by Picasso's painting). The rebuilt town has an excellent food market and good restaurants. The Casa de Juntas (Basque Parliament) and peace museum are worth an hour. Low-key, unhurried, and genuinely interesting.
🕛 12:00 – 15:30
🌊
Afternoon — back to Laga or explore the estuary
Another swim at Laga. Or drive the 15 km to Laida beach on the opposite side of the estuary for a completely different feel — calmer water, views back across to the mountains, a tiny beach bar.
🕔 Afternoon drift
💡 Gernika market day is Monday — if Day 3 falls on a Monday, the market at the Plaza de Europa is one of the best in the Basque Country. Vegetables, cheese, flowers, honey, and local food from surrounding villages.
🗺 Google Maps
4Day
San Sebastián — La Concha & Parte Vieja
🚐 ~90 km 🏖 Best city beach 🍢 Culinary capital
▾
🏖
Playa de La Concha
Consistently ranked among Europe's most beautiful city beaches. A perfect crescent of fine sand, sheltered and calm, with an elegant promenade. Rated 4.7⭐ with 23,000+ reviews. The small island (Isla de Santa Clara) in the bay is reachable by small boat in summer. Swim, sunbathe — the city behind provides a dramatic backdrop. Come in the morning before crowds build.
🕙 09:30 – 12:00
🍢
Parte Vieja Pintxos Crawl
San Sebastián has more Michelin stars per square kilometre than anywhere in the world outside Japan. The Parte Vieja bars are extraordinary. Key stops: Bar Néstor (legendary tortilla — arrive at 12:45 to put your name on the list, they make exactly two per day, served at 13:00 and 19:00), Borda Berri (creative, outstanding), Gandarias (traditional, reliable). Budget €30–40 per person.
🕐 13:00 – 16:30
⛰
Monte Igueldo — Golden Hour View
Ride the vintage funicular up the hill for the definitive panorama of La Concha bay. Going at golden hour (around 20:00 in summer) is something special — the whole bay turns orange. There's a slightly retro amusement park at the top that's charmingly old-fashioned.
🕗 19:30 – 21:00 · Funicular €4 return
🚐
Camping Jaizkibel (east of San Sebastián)
Forested hilltop campsite east of the city, 15 minutes from the Parte Vieja. Good facilities, mountain views, quiet. Heading east from here tomorrow is straightforward.
🕙 Late evening · ~€22–28/night
🚐 Urdaibai → San Sebastián: ~90 km, 1h 10min 📌 Park the van outside the old town — city centre is not van-friendly
💡 Bar Néstor tortilla: One of the most famous tortillas in Spain. They make exactly two per day — one at 13:00, one at 19:00. Go to the bar at 12:45, put your name on the list (the barman writes it down), and come back at 13:00. Don't be late. It's worth it.
🗺 Google Maps
5Day
Getaria & Zumaia Flysch Coast
🚐 ~60 km 🐟 Grilled fish 🪨 Geology
▾
⚓
Getaria Fishing Village
A picture-perfect medieval fishing village — cobbled streets, a quirky Gothic church with a sloped floor, and the best open-fire grilled fish on the Basque coast. Rated 4.6⭐. Birthplace of Juan Sebastián Elcano, the first person to circumnavigate the globe. The asadores (charcoal grill houses) on the harbourfront are exceptional — order rodaballo (turbot) or txipirón (squid) grilled whole. The Balenciaga Museum is nearby (€10). Allow at least a half day here — don't rush it.
🕙 10:00 – 14:30 · Long lunch included
🪨
Zumaia — Flysch Cliffs UNESCO Geopark
The Flysch of Zumaia is a geological marvel — 60 million years of Earth's history written in dramatic tilted rock strata rising vertically from the sea. The strata are so clearly defined that the K-Pg boundary (the mass extinction that killed the dinosaurs) is visible as a thin dark line in the cliff face. Best seen by boat — the guided trip from Zumaia is excellent (geoparkea.eus, ~€15). Also featured in Game of Thrones.
🕒 15:30 – 17:30 · Boat tour ~€15
🚐
Camp near Deba or push south toward La Rioja
Camping Deba is a solid stop on the Basque coast. Alternatively, if energy allows, push south on the A-15 toward Logroño — you'd arrive in La Rioja territory for the evening, ready for a full wine country day tomorrow.
🕗 19:00 · ~€18–22/night
🚐 San Sebastián → Getaria: ~28 km, 30 min 📍 Getaria → Zumaia: 10 km, 10 min 📍 Zumaia → Deba: 10 km, 12 min
🗺 Google Maps
6Day
Into La Rioja — Haro & Logroño
🍷 Wine country 🚐 ~140 km south 🏛 Tapas capital
▾
🍷
Haro — Bodega Visit
Haro is the wine capital of La Rioja Alta — the highest-quality Rioja zone. The "Barrio de la Estación" (Station Quarter) contains several grand 19th-century bodegas within walking distance of each other. Bodegas Gómez Cruzado (4.9⭐, 820 reviews) runs excellent private tours with tastings — "exceptional... exactly how wine tourism should be." Bodegas La Rioja Alta (4.6⭐, 752 reviews) is the grande dame — 130-year-old cellars, older vintages available. Book ahead for both.
🕙 10:30 – 13:30 · Tours ~€15–25 per person · Book at gomezcruzado.com
🍢
Logroño — Calle Laurel Tapas Street
Logroño is one of Spain's great tapas cities, and Calle Laurel is its nerve centre — 50+ bars in a few concentrated blocks. Rated 4.6⭐ with 4,600+ reviews. The method here is the same as pintxos but more casual: go from bar to bar, order the house speciality at each, drink the local Rioja. Famous stops: Bar Soriano (fried mushroom with prawn, that's it, that's their thing), Bar Blanco y Negro, Taberna del Tío Blas.
🕒 14:30 – 17:30 · ~25 km from Haro
🚐
Camping La Grana (near Logroño)
Well-equipped campsite outside Logroño in the Rioja countryside. Surrounded by vineyards. Good base for tonight and tomorrow's Laguardia visit.
🕗 19:00 · ~€18–22/night
🚐 Deba → Haro: ~145 km, 1h 35min 📍 Haro → Logroño: 40 km, 35 min 📌 A-12/N-232 south through wine country — vines as far as you can see
💡 Rioja wine: Rioja is aged wine — Crianza (min. 2 years), Reserva (min. 3), Gran Reserva (min. 5). At bodega tastings, ask specifically for older Gran Reserva vintages — many bodegas have back-stock they rarely advertise. The 2004, 2010, and 2016 vintages are considered exceptional.
🗺 Google Maps
7Day
Laguardia — Medieval Wine Village
🍷 Rioja Alavesa 🏰 Walled village ~35 km from Logroño
▾
🏰
Laguardia — Walled Medieval Village
Rated 4.7⭐ with 6,700+ reviews — "charming, charming, charming." A beautifully preserved walled medieval hilltop town in the middle of the Rioja Alavesa wine region (the Basque Country's wine zone). The views over the vineyards toward the Sierra de Cantabria are extraordinary. Free car park just outside the walls. The town is small — cobbled streets, ancient gateways, a musical clock, excellent tapas bars. The underground cave network beneath the village (running 5km) was used for centuries to keep wine cool — guided tours are available.
🕙 09:30 – 13:30 · Free to visit · Cave tours ~€8
🏛
Marqués de Riscal Winery & Gehry Building
Just outside Laguardia in Elciego is the extraordinary Marqués de Riscal winery — with a Frank Gehry-designed hotel and visitor centre made of twisted titanium ribbons (same architect as the Guggenheim). The winery tour and tasting is superb. Reviewers call it "one of the most stunning architectural achievements" in the Rioja. Book ahead — marquesderiscal.com.
🕒 14:30 – 17:00 · Tour + tasting ~€20–35 · 10 min from Laguardia
🚐
Camp near Laguardia or head back north toward Bilbao

Playa de Laredo — La Salvé
A vast 4.5 km sweep of fine white sand — one of the longest beaches in Cantabria, rated 4.6⭐. Faces the open Bay of Biscay, excellent surf, and cleaned daily in summer. Bilbao Airport is 10 km from the city centre — a striking Calatrava-designed terminal. Direct flights to Dublin, London, Edinburgh and other European cities. Campervan hire companies generally have a return point at or near the airport — confirm this when booking the van. Allow 2–3 hours for van return, check-in, and security.

Campervan Essentials

  • Book 2–3 months ahead for summer. Try Yescapa, McRent, or Avis Motorhomes
  • For 4 people: a 6-berth van or a 4-berth fixed-bed layout is ideal. Confirm the bed configuration — a fixed double + fixed bunk is more liveable than a dinette conversion
  • Download Park4Night app before leaving — essential for finding camper areas, wild spots, and honest reviews
  • Wild camping: technically legal if you don't deploy levelling legs, table, or chairs outside the van. In practice, use the red-dot área de autocaravanas — cheap (€8–16/night) and excellent
  • Fuel: fill at Carrefour, Alcampo, or Repsol motorway stations. LPG is widely available in Asturias and Galicia
📅

When to Go

  • June: Ideal. Warm, long days, green hills at their peak, far fewer crowds. Sea ~17–18°C
  • July–August: Warmest. Sea ~20°C. Everything is busier and pricier — book everything in advance. Worth it if July/August is the only option
  • September: Often the best month — harvest season, warm sea, thinner crowds, excellent food
  • Rain can come at any time in Green Spain — pack a waterproof and layers even in summer
  • Sunset is after 22:00 in July. Dinner before 21:00 marks you as a tourist — embrace late eating
🏖

Beach Safety

  • Northern Spain beaches have Atlantic swell. Always check the flag: 🟢 safe, 🟡 caution, 🔴 no swimming
  • Rip currents are common on exposed surf beaches (A Frouxeira, Laga, Berria). Swim between the flag poles where lifeguards are stationed
  • Water temperatures: Galicia ~16–18°C even in August. Basque coast ~19–21°C in August. Cold by Med standards — refreshing for swimming, worthwhile for surfing with a 3/2mm wetsuit
  • Cíes Islands: no equipment rental on site. Bring your own snorkel mask — the water is crystal clear
🍽

Food & Drink

  • Menú del día: €10–16 for 3 courses with wine at lunch. Available Mon–Fri at most restaurants, 13:00–15:30. Best value in Spain
  • Pintxos/pinchos etiquette: take from the bar, keep your cocktail sticks, pay by counting them at the end. Don't ask what everything is — just point and eat
  • Wines to know: Albariño (Galicia, white, crisp), Txakoli (Basque, lightly sparkling, with grilled fish), Rioja Reserva (red, aged), Ribeira Sacra Mencía (red, earthy)
  • Mercadona supermarkets everywhere — excellent and affordable for van stocking. Their Hacendado wine range is outstanding value
  • Sidra (Asturian cider) — served at room temperature, poured from height. Always say yes
