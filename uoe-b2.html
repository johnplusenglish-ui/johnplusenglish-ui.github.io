Part 1 🔘 Multiple Choice Cloze
Part 1 — Multiple Choice Cloze. Read the text and choose the best answer (A, B, C or D) for each gap. Eight questions, 1 mark each.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 1▾
Strategy
💡Read the whole text first for overall meaning. Then read each gapped sentence carefully before looking at the options.
💡Many questions test collocations (words that naturally go together). Think: does this word "sound right" with the words around it?
Common Mistakes
⚠️All four options may be grammatically correct. The difference is often which word collocates with the context.
⚠️Don't translate from your language — English collocations are often different from what you'd expect.
Time
⏱️Aim for about 10 minutes. If stuck, eliminate the options you're sure are wrong and make your best choice.

Part 2 ✏️ Open Cloze
Part 2 — Open Cloze. Read the text and think of the word which best fits each gap. Use only one word in each gap.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 2▾
Strategy
💡Most answers are grammar words: prepositions, conjunctions, pronouns, articles, auxiliaries, or quantifiers.
💡Look for fixed phrases and collocations: "in spite of", "as well as", "due to", "apart from".
Common Mistakes
⚠️Only ONE word per gap. Contractions like "don't" count as two words.
⚠️Check your answer fits grammatically with the words on BOTH sides of the gap.
Time
⏱️Aim for about 10 minutes. Fill in the confident answers first, then return to harder ones.

Part 3 🔤 Word Formation
Part 3 — Word Formation. Read the text below. Use the word given in capitals at the end of some of the lines to form a word that fits in the gap in the same line.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 3▾
Strategy
💡Decide what part of speech is needed: noun, adjective, adverb, or verb. Then consider whether it's positive or negative.
💡Check whether you need a negative prefix (un-, in-, im-, dis-), a plural, or a compound change (e.g. EMPLOY → unemployment).
Common Mistakes
⚠️Spelling matters! Double-check tricky suffixes: -tion/-sion, -ence/-ance, -ible/-able.
⚠️Some words need two changes: a prefix AND a suffix. Read the full sentence to check the meaning.
Time
⏱️About 10 minutes. If stuck, write your best guess — a blank always scores zero.

Part 4 🔑 Key Word Transformations
Part 4 — Key Word Transformations. Complete the second sentence so that it has a similar meaning to the first, using the word given. Do not change the word given.
📝 Exam Tips — Part 4▾
Strategy
💡The key word must NOT be changed in any way — no adding -s, -ed, -ing.
💡Identify the grammar being tested: passive, conditional, reported speech, phrasal verb, comparison, etc.
Common Mistakes
⚠️Count your words! The answer must be between 3 and 6 words including the key word.
⚠️Both sentences must have exactly the same meaning. Don't add or lose information.
Time
⏱️About 12–15 minutes. Part 4 is often the hardest, so invest the time.
