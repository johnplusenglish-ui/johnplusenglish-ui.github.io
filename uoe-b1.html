Part 1 Multiple Choice Cloze
Read the text and choose the best answer (A, B, C or D) for each gap. Eight questions, 1 mark each.
Tips▾
Strategy
💡Read the whole text first to understand the general meaning before looking at the options.
💡Look at the words before and after each gap. The answer often depends on which words go together naturally.
Common Mistakes
⚠️All four options may look similar. Check which one fits the meaning AND the grammar of the sentence.
⚠️Don't choose an answer just because it looks like a word in your language — check the context.
Time
⏱️Spend about 8–10 minutes on this part. If a question is too hard, skip it and come back.

Part 2 Open Cloze
Read the text and think of the word which best fits each gap. Use only one word in each gap.
Tips▾
Strategy
💡Most answers are small grammar words: prepositions (in, on, at), articles (a, the), pronouns (it, which), or conjunctions (but, and).
💡Read the whole sentence before and after the gap. The answer usually makes a common phrase or pattern.
Common Mistakes
⚠️Only ONE word per gap. Don't write two words or a contraction like "don't" (that counts as two).
⚠️Check spelling carefully. Even a small mistake means zero marks for that gap.
Time
⏱️About 8–10 minutes. Fill in the easy gaps first, then go back to the hard ones.

Part 3 Word Formation
Read the text below. Use the word given in capitals at the end of some of the lines to form a word that fits in the gap in the same line.
Tips▾
Strategy
💡First, decide what type of word you need: a noun, adjective, adverb, or verb.
💡Check if you need a negative form (un-, dis-, im-) or a plural (-s). Read the sentence carefully for clues.
Common Mistakes
⚠️Spelling is very important! Common endings like -tion, -ment, -ful, and -ness must be exact.
⚠️Don't just add a suffix — sometimes you need to change the base word too (e.g. decide → decision).
Time
⏱️About 8–10 minutes. If you're stuck, write your best guess — a blank always scores zero.

Part 4 Key Word Transformations
Complete the second sentence so that it has a similar meaning to the first, using the word given. Do not change the word given.
Tips▾
Strategy
💡The key word must NOT be changed — don't add -s, -ed, -ing, or anything else to it.
💡Think about what grammar is being tested: passive voice, reported speech, conditionals, comparatives, or phrasal verbs.
Common Mistakes
⚠️Count your words! You must use between 3 and 6 words, including the key word.
⚠️Both sentences must mean EXACTLY the same thing. Don't add or lose any information.
Time
⏱️About 10–12 minutes. This is often the hardest part, so give yourself enough time.
